## Verdict

**SUPPORTED.** On Tuesday, April 28, 2026, at HESA Meeting 31, Liberal Parliamentary Secretary of Health Maggie Chi moved a motion "That the committee proceed to sit in camera." The motion carried 6–5 on a strict party-line vote (6 Liberals YEA, 4 Conservatives plus 1 Bloc NAY) at 12:16 p.m., suspending the public webcast. The motion was moved while Conservative MP Dan Mazier had a motion on the floor to ask the Auditor General to audit PrescribeIT and to summon the Minister of Health. A parallel in-camera vote occurred the same morning at the Standing Committee on Access to Information, Privacy and Ethics (ETHI), where Liberal members shut down debate on a Conservative motion that would have required the Privy Council Office to provide regular updates on the prime minister's ethics screen.

The Apr 28 prior brief was correct as of the evening it was compiled — at that point HESA Meeting 31 minutes were not yet posted and no major outlet had run a write-up. Within roughly 12 hours, multiple outlets did. We can write this article.

- **URL:** https://www.ourcommons.ca/DocumentViewer/en/45-1/HESA/meeting-31/minutes
- **Format:** Public minutes of the Standing Committee on Health, 45th Parliament, 1st Session
- **Date of meeting:** Tuesday, April 28, 2026, 12:00 p.m. – 1:00 p.m.

**Members present:**
- Liberal: Maggie Chi (Don Valley North, Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister of Health), Sukh Dhaliwal, Doug Eyolfson, Helena Jaczek, Aslam Rana, Sonia Sidhu
- Conservative: Burton Bailey, Helena Konanz, Dan Mazier, Matt Strauss
- Bloc Québécois: Maxime Blanchette-Joncas
- Acting (Liberal): Chris Bittle (for Hedy Fry), Jake Sawatzky (for Aslam Rana)

**Motions and votes:**

- **Motion 1 (Mazier):** That the committee request the Auditor General audit the PrescribeIT program; and invite the Minister of Health and officials to appear for two hours within two weeks.

- **Motion 2 (Chi):** "That the committee proceed to sit in camera."

**Vote on Motion 2 (in camera):**
- YEAS (6): Bittle, Chi, Eyolfson, Jaczek, Sawatzky, Sidhu — all Liberal
- NAYS (5): Bailey, Blanchette-Joncas, Konanz, Mazier, Strauss — 4 Conservative + 1 Bloc
- Result: **CARRIED 6–5 on a strict party-line vote.**

**Timeline:**
- 12:16 p.m.: meeting suspended (cameras off)
- 12:24 p.m.: resumed in camera
- 12:36–12:42, 12:51–12:56: additional in-camera suspensions
- Meeting concluded with committee ordering unredacted documents from Canada Health Infoway and Telus Health on PrescribeIT costs, governance, contracts, board minutes, and expenses (despite the in-camera segment, the documents were ultimately ordered)

This is a confirmed in-camera vote on a public-interest matter, on a Liberal-party-line, on the same day the Liberals' new House majority took effect. It is documented in an official ourcommons.ca minutes record. **This is the strongest possible source class.**

## Note on the prior research

The April 28 briefs (`research-prescribeit.md` and `research-in-camera-hearing.md`) reached a careful, defensible conclusion at the time they were written: the in-camera framing could not be confirmed because (a) the HESA Meeting 31 minutes were not yet published, (b) no major outlet had run a write-up, and (c) the Conservative Party's own April 27 press release used "filibustered" not "in camera." The cautious editorial decision to publish the April 29 PrescribeIT article on the spending angle without the in-camera frame was correct.

What changed in the seven days since:
1. HESA Meeting 31 minutes were posted publicly with the named 6–5 vote.
2. Canadian Press ran a full wire story on April 29 with on-the-record Conservative quotes including Mazier's "voted to shut down the cameras."
3. CBC, Global News, National Post, and others followed up.
4. A second committee (ETHI) followed the same pattern the same morning, establishing it as strategy rather than incident.

The reframe is now defensible. We can — and should — write the article.
