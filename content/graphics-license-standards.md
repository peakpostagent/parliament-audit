# Graphics + License Standards

What images we use, where we get them, and how we credit them. Read this before adding any image to an article or social post.

## The principle

Parliament Audit is a non-commercial civic-news outlet. Every image we publish must satisfy at least one of:

1. **Crown copyright with explicit non-commercial reproduction permission** (House of Commons of Canada / Parliament Library / Open Government Canada).
2. **A free Creative Commons license** that allows our use case (CC0, CC-BY, CC-BY-SA — all compatible with us).
3. **A free-for-any-use stock-photo license** (Unsplash, Pexels, Pixabay).
4. **Generated by us** server-side via our `/api/og/*` routes — comparison cards, charts, branded headers.

If an image doesn't fit one of those four buckets, we don't publish it.

## The good list — what we can use

### Government of Canada (Crown copyright, non-commercial OK)

| Source | URL pattern | Best for | Attribution |
|---|---|---|---|
| Official MP portraits | `https://www.ourcommons.ca/Members/en/<slug>(<id>)/photo` | MP face cards, comparison cards | "Photo: House of Commons of Canada" |
| Senator portraits | Library of Parliament — `lop.parl.ca` | Senate coverage | "Photo: Library of Parliament" |
| Parliamentary committee video stills | `parlvu.parl.gc.ca` | Witnesses, testimony | "Image: ParlVU / Parliament of Canada" |
| Open Government image library | `open.canada.ca` | Government scenes, infographics | "Government of Canada — Open Government Licence" |
| Government of Canada websites generally | `canada.ca` Crown-copyrighted images | Generic government branding | "Government of Canada" |

**The rule:** Crown copyright permits reproduction "without permission" for non-commercial purposes including news reporting, education, satire, and parody. Source: [Reproduction of Government of Canada Works](https://www.canada.ca/en/transparency/terms.html). Commercial use needs written permission — not us, but worth knowing.

**Attribution placement:** in the OG card's bottom band ("Photos: House of Commons of Canada"), in article alt text, and in the article body if the photo is editorially load-bearing.

### Wikimedia Commons (free licenses)

URL pattern: `commons.wikimedia.org/wiki/File:<filename>`. Search by politician name. Most Canadian MPs / ministers / premiers / opposition leaders have at least one free-licensed photo on Commons.

Each photo's license is shown at the bottom of its Commons page. Only use ones marked:

- `Public domain` (no attribution required, but we credit anyway as good practice)
- `CC0` (no attribution required, but credit anyway)
- `CC-BY` (must credit photographer + license)
- `CC-BY-SA` (must credit photographer + license + share-alike — i.e. we license our reuse the same way)

Avoid: any "non-commercial only" license (CC-BY-NC etc). Wikimedia mostly avoids these, but they exist.

**Attribution example for CC-BY-SA:** `Photo: Erin Schaff / CC-BY-SA 4.0`.

### Stock-photo libraries (free for any use)

| Site | License | Attribution | Best for |
|---|---|---|---|
| [Unsplash](https://unsplash.com) | Unsplash License (free, commercial OK, no credit required) | Optional — credit anyway when natural | Parliament Hill exterior, flags, polling stations, civic backgrounds |
| [Pexels](https://www.pexels.com) | Pexels License (free, commercial OK) | Optional | Same as above |
| [Pixabay](https://pixabay.com) | Pixabay Content License (free, commercial OK) | Optional | Same as above |

These are useful for header images on explainer articles, the "Find Your MP" page hero, and other generic civic imagery. They are not useful for identifying specific Canadian politicians — that's what ourcommons.ca and Wikimedia are for.

### Our own generators

| Endpoint | Output | When to use |
|---|---|---|
| `/api/og/news/<slug>` | Default headline-stat card with category badge | Standard articles, where there's a hero number to extract |
| `/api/og/comparison?slug=<slug>` | Split-portrait card with two subjects | Articles with `subjects: [...]` in news-articles.ts (floor-crossings, before/after, contradictions) |
| `/api/og/chart?type=...` | Branded data-viz card | Vote-result charts, polling, budget breakdowns |
| `/api/og/banner` | 1500×500 X profile banner | Brand identity (mostly run-once) |

For the comparison card to render with portraits, the article's `subjects[].portraitUrl` must point at a publicly-accessible image. ourcommons.ca portrait URLs work directly.

## The bad list — what we don't use

| Source | Why not |
|---|---|
| CBC / CTV / Global / Globe and Mail / Postmedia photos | Copyrighted. Fair-use coverage in Canada is narrow ("fair dealing" — research, private study, education, parody, satire, criticism, review, news reporting). News reporting fair dealing covers brief direct quotation in a press review, but does not cover us using their photo as our own image. Ask: would they license it? Then it's not fair dealing. |
| Reuters / AP / AFP wire photos | Same — wire-service photos are licensed per use. We don't have a license. |
| Getty Images, Shutterstock | Commercially licensed only. Watermarks are common for unlicensed use. |
| Random Twitter/X images | Unknown provenance. Even if visible publicly, the copyright belongs to whoever took the photo. |
| AI-generated images of real politicians | Disqualifying for a non-partisan brand. AI-generated faces of real people misrepresent reality. Don't. |
| Memes | Same issues — unknown provenance, often copyrighted source material, plus the partisan-positioning risk. |

## Format conventions for our images

### OG cards (our generators)

- 1200 × 630 (X / Facebook standard)
- Dark background (`#0D1117`) — matches our brand
- Red accent (`#D71920`) for category labels and CTAs
- Bottom band always carries `PARLIAMENTAUDIT.CA` + attribution
- Headline ≤ 110 characters (truncates if longer)

### Inline article images

Currently not used systematically. If we add inline images:

- Width: 800px max (HiDPI: serve 2x at 1600px, downscale)
- Format: WebP first, with JPG fallback
- Required: `alt=""` (empty if decorative) or descriptive alt text
- Required: figure caption with photographer + license

### Social-post attached images (not the OG card — explicit attached images on Bluesky / X)

Currently we don't attach images to posts; we let the OG card render via the link preview. If we change that approach later (carousel posts à la 401_da_sarpanch), the image must be:

- 1200 × 1200 (square, plays well across X / Bluesky / LinkedIn)
- Generated via `/api/og/comparison` with `?format=square` (would require a route extension — not built yet)
- Re-render before each post so cache busting isn't required

## Auditing

Once a quarter, run a content audit:

1. Check every OG card on every news article renders correctly (image not 404)
2. Check every inline article image still has its license attribution
3. Re-validate Wikimedia Commons photo licenses haven't been revised — they occasionally are
4. Check Crown copyright photos still resolve (rare, but ourcommons.ca portrait URLs change when MPs leave office)

Currently no automation for this. If we have inline images deployed at scale, build a `scripts/audit-images.ts` step.

## When in doubt

If a contributor proposes an image and you can't immediately classify it under one of the four good-list buckets above: don't publish it. Civic-news credibility is built on accurate sourcing. An image we can't license cleanly is an own-goal waiting to happen.
