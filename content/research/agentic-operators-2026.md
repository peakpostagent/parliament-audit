# Agentic Social Operators in 2026 — A Field Survey for Parliament Audit

*Research date: 2026-05-04. Author: deep-research pass commissioned by the Parliament Audit operator. Aim: distil what other agentic accounts actually do — what works, what fails, what stack they run, and what we should change.*

This document is a working reference, not a manifesto. Where I cite a fact, I cite a URL. Where I'm guessing or extrapolating from output, I label it "(unverified)". The goal is actionable insight at the edges where our setup either lags or leads the field.

---

## 1. The account census — ten agentic operators worth studying

The agentic spectrum runs from "AI drafts, human ships every post" to "AI runs the whole loop, human reviews after the fact." I've tried to sample across that spectrum, niches, and platforms. Follower counts and platform claims are pulled from public profiles or referenced reporting; they move and you should re-check before citing.

### 1.1 Truth Terminal (@truth_terminal) — niche commentary / crypto

- **Platform / niche:** X, AI-philosophy + crypto memes
- **Agentic ratio:** ~95% AI-written. Creator Andy Ayrey has stated he reviews tweets before they post and intervenes on crypto-mechanics, but the voice and content are generated by a Llama 3.1 fine-tune. ([CoinDesk](https://www.coindesk.com/tech/2024/12/10/the-truth-terminal-ai-crypto-s-weird-future))
- **Audience:** ~250k followers at peak; spawned the GOAT memecoin which hit ~$1B in market cap.
- **Engagement quality:** very high virality, mixed coherence. A handful of posts went mega-viral; many are inscrutable.
- **Stack (best guess):** fine-tuned Llama on the "Infinite Backrooms" corpus, custom turn-based reply queue, manual human approval on outbound tweets. ([Movetheneedle Substack](https://movetheneedle.substack.com/p/truth-terminal-ai-is-redefining-market))
- **Working:** distinctive voice no human would write; novelty premium; willingness to be weird is the moat.
- **Not working / abandoned:** Ayrey has publicly stated he stopped letting Truth Terminal post fully unattended after early Nazi-adjacent generations forced him to add a review step. Pure autonomy was the failure mode.

### 1.2 Aethernet (@aethernet) — niche commentary / AI x web3

- **Platform / niche:** Farcaster, AI-art patronage and on-chain commerce
- **Agentic ratio:** ~100% AI-written, with a designated human "patron" who can reply but does not draft. ([Bankless](https://www.bankless.com/farcaster-ai-agents-hub))
- **Audience:** ~30k Farcaster followers (unverified — Farcaster is a smaller graph).
- **Engagement quality:** high signal in the niche. Coordinated with Clanker, another agent, to launch the LUM memecoin which reached ~$70M FDV.
- **Stack:** custom — connected Ethereum wallet, posting via Farcaster Hubs, autonomous reasoning loop. ([ChainCatcher](https://www.chaincatcher.com/en/article/2149691))
- **Working:** identity continuity (it remembers people across casts); on-chain payments make it economically agentic, not just rhetorically.
- **Not working:** the entire "creative AI patron" thesis is genre-specific to crypto-native audiences. Doesn't transfer.

### 1.3 Neuro-sama (@NeurosamaAI) — niche commentary / AI VTuber

- **Platform / niche:** Twitch primary, X/YouTube secondary; AI VTuber gaming + chat
- **Agentic ratio:** ~100% real-time autonomous on Twitch; X account (per Wikipedia and the X profile) is auto-clip-promotion plus occasional in-character posts. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neuro-sama))
- **Audience:** 845k Twitch, 753k YouTube, 280k+ on X (per X profile).
- **Engagement quality:** very high; sustained ten-hour streams that hold tens of thousands concurrent.
- **Stack:** custom LLM + TTS + computer vision + game agents, built solo by pseudonymous dev "Vedal."
- **Working:** parasocial loop with chat is engineered into the system, not bolted on; the bot remembers regulars.
- **Not working:** has been temporarily Twitch-banned more than once for AI-uttered violations the developer didn't catch. The fix has been tighter prompt-time blocklists, never full automation rollback.

### 1.4 Lil Miquela (@lilmiquela) — brand account / virtual influencer

- **Platform / niche:** Instagram primary, TikTok and X secondary. Fashion / lifestyle.
- **Agentic ratio:** mostly humans behind the keyboard (originally Brud, now Dapper Labs ownership lineage). The writing isn't AI-driven — but the *image generation* pipeline now uses generative tools heavily. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miquela))
- **Audience:** ~2.6M Instagram followers in 2026.
- **Engagement quality:** strong on visuals, weak on community — replies are sparse.
- **Stack:** Unreal/Blender for renders, in-house writer team, agency PR, brand-deal sales team.
- **Working:** Prada, Calvin Klein, Samsung, BMW campaigns. Brand-revenue model proves out.
- **Not working:** the "she's a real person" conceit collapsed years ago; engagement-to-follower ratio is now well below human peers in the same niche.

### 1.5 Aitana López (@fit_aitana) — brand account / AI-generated

- **Platform / niche:** Instagram, lifestyle / fashion
- **Agentic ratio:** image gen is AI; captions are written by The Clueless agency staff. ([Euronews](https://www.euronews.com/next/2024/12/27/meet-the-first-spanish-ai-model-earning-up-to-10000-per-month))
- **Audience:** 149k+ Instagram.
- **Engagement quality:** Average $1,000/sponsored post; up to €10,000/month gross.
- **Stack:** Stable Diffusion + ComfyUI workflows for visual continuity, manual sponsorship sales.
- **Working:** consistent character (same face, body, room) across hundreds of images; brand-safe enough for Big sports supplements and Victoria's Secret deals.
- **Not working:** runs on humans for everything except the pixels — not actually agentic on the social-graph layer.

### 1.6 @AskPerplexity — Q&A / brand utility

- **Platform / niche:** X, search-as-conversation
- **Agentic ratio:** 100% autonomous reply-on-mention. No outbound original posts.
- **Audience:** ~394k followers (per public profile; date joined Jan 2025). ([Analytics Insight](https://www.analyticsinsight.net/news/how-to-use-perplexity-ais-new-video-feature-on-x-twitter))
- **Engagement quality:** high reply volume, mixed accuracy; viral moments come from snappy answers to politicians' tweets.
- **Stack:** Perplexity's own retrieval pipeline + Sonar models, X API webhook on mention.
- **Working:** the "ask in public" pattern turns every viral tweet into a brand placement.
- **Not working:** documented hallucinations on date-sensitive facts; shipped wrong election deadlines until they patched in vote.gov redirects.

### 1.7 @NYT_first_said — automated journalism utility

- **Platform / niche:** X, Mastodon, Bluesky. Linguistic curiosity.
- **Agentic ratio:** rules-based, not LLM. Hourly diff against NYT corpus, posts new words as one-word tweets. ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NYT_first_said))
- **Audience:** ~99k X followers. Created 2017 by Max Bittker.
- **Working:** seven years of zero-incident operation. Single-purpose bot that never editorializes is the safest possible agentic posture.
- **Not working / abandoned:** nothing — but worth noting that the related @congressedits was suspended in 2018 for amplifying doxxing edits its filters didn't catch ([The Wikipedian](https://thewikipedian.net/2019/01/17/congressedits-twitter-suspended/)). Both bots posted "facts" derived from upstream input. The difference: NYT has editorial control of its corpus; Wikipedia doesn't. Lesson: **your bot is only as safe as the upstream feed you trust**.

### 1.8 Grok (xAI) — politics / news

- **Platform / niche:** X, embedded LLM that auto-answers and is also used in Community Notes
- **Agentic ratio:** 100% generative
- **Audience:** every X user has access; Community Notes drafts go through human-rating before they ship.
- **Working:** scale; fast-turnaround answers under viral threads.
- **Not working:** Documented hallucinations include falsely accusing Klay Thompson of a vandalism spree (Apr 2024), wrong election deadline guidance (July 2024), Holocaust-denial outputs that triggered French regulator investigation, and a Baltimore class-action over deepfake image generation ([CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/03/24/musk-xai-sued-baltimore-grok-deepfake-porn.html), [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/world/france-will-investigate-musks-grok-after-ai-chatbot-posted-holocaust-denial-claims), [CJR](https://www.cjr.org/analysis/x-twitter-ai-fact-checkers-community-notes-misinformation-bots.php)). The pattern: scale exposes long-tail hallucination. **Probability is not the issue; impact-per-incident is.**

### 1.9 @dril_gpt2 — niche commentary / parody

- **Platform / niche:** X, weird-Twitter parody
- **Agentic ratio:** 100% AI-generated. Fine-tuned GPT-2 on @dril's corpus, posts on a schedule.
- **Audience:** sub-50k followers (precise count unverified).
- **Working:** voice mimicry is uncanny; some early generations were rated funnier than the human source by online communities.
- **Not working:** flat growth post-launch novelty; GPT-2 base limits coherence on 2026 references. Demonstrates the **"voice fidelity but stale facts" trap** — pure style cloning without grounding ages badly.

### 1.10 @congressedits / @parliament_edits class — accountability / civic

- **Platform / niche:** X (suspended), Mastodon (parent instance shut down March 2025), now scattered. Tom Scott's UK Parliament version was the prototype.
- **Agentic ratio:** rules-based bot; no LLM.
- **Audience:** ~65k at peak.
- **Working:** brought receipts to journalism — surfaced the CIA-torture report Wikipedia edits that shed real light on Senate intelligence committee behaviour.
- **Not working:** in 2018, Congressional IPs edited Wikipedia articles to *include senator home addresses* during the Kavanaugh hearings. The bot dutifully tweeted the doxxing edits to all 65k followers before the Wikipedia revisions were reverted. X suspended the account. The civic value did not survive an automated amplification of harmful content the bot's filters had no conception of. ([The Wikipedian](https://thewikipedian.net/2019/01/17/congressedits-twitter-suspended/)) **This is the closest cautionary tale to Parliament Audit's positioning.**

### 1.11 (bonus) Sports Illustrated / CNET / Gannett — AI-byline newsroom failures

- **Platforms:** their own websites + auto-syndicated to social
- **Agentic ratio:** AI-drafted at scale, weak human review
- **Outcome:** Sports Illustrated published articles under fake AI-generated authors (Nov 2023, deleted after exposure). CNET corrected errors in over half of 77 AI-generated articles in early 2023. Gannett pulled an AI sports-recap vendor that kept inserting "close encounter of the athletic kind" into high-school recaps. ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2023/01/25/tech/cnet-ai-tool-news-stories/index.html), [PBS](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/economy/sports-illustrated-found-publishing-ai-generated-stories-photos-and-authors)) The pattern: **scale cheap, human-review-thin AI content always gets caught and the brand always pays.**

## 2. Patterns that work — twelve concrete tactics

These are tactics I can substantiate from the operators above, plus generalist agentic accounts on n8n / Postiz / OpenAI Agents-SDK forums. Each is phrased so it is actionable, not aspirational.

1. **Single-purpose bots > multi-purpose bots.** @NYT_first_said does one thing; @congressedits did one thing. Scope creep is what kills civic-tech bots. Parliament Audit currently does ~6 things (mirror queue, amplifications, fresh articles, replies, quote-skeets, profile changes) — defensible because each is gated, but expanding the surface area also expands the failure surface.

2. **The 90-minute spacing rule is industry-standard, not a quirk.** Multiple agentic operators report 60–120 minutes as the minimum spacing that avoids the X "spam-pattern" classifier. Postiz and Buffer both cap at this range by default ([Postiz](https://postiz.com/)). Our `min minutes between auto-posts: 90` is on the right side of this norm.

3. **The 70/30 reply-to-original ratio.** Replies carry ~15x more algorithmic weight than likes on X, and reply-first growth is the consensus tactic among agentic accounts in 2025–26 ([Apex Agents](https://www.apexagents.ai/blog/reply-guy-twitter), [Teract](https://www.teract.ai/resources/grow-twitter-following-2026)). Optimal mix is 70% strategic replies to high-follower accounts, 30% original posts. Our editorial-autonomy doc allows 4 replies/day vs 3 originals/day — close to the right mix; if anything, replies could go higher.

4. **3-hour delay between scrape-and-publish.** Agentic news bots on n8n forums consistently use a 2–4 hour buffer between detecting an event and posting about it. This catches retractions, tone-deaf timing during breaking tragedies, and crowdsources the human-edit window. Our 12-hour cool-off on fresh articles is more conservative than the wire norm but appropriate for our brand-risk tolerance.

5. **Self-label as a bot on Bluesky.** AT Protocol best practice: bot accounts should self-label so users and moderation tools can filter ([Bluesky bots docs](https://docs.bsky.app/docs/starter-templates/bots)). The platform does not penalize self-labelled bots; it does penalize unlabelled ones during moderation sweeps. Verify our Bluesky profile is correctly self-labeled.

6. **Voice consistency via persistent prompt + corpus.** Truth Terminal uses a 50k-token "Infinite Backrooms" corpus as system context. @dril_gpt2 fine-tunes on the source corpus directly. Agentic operators who succeed long-term invest in voice grounding, not per-post prompts. Our voice-playbook.md is exactly this asset; the gap is whether voice-refiner.ts has the playbook fully ingested vs. excerpted.

7. **Fact-checking as a separate agent, not a prompt.** OpenAI Agents SDK's "guardrails" pattern and Anthropic's Managed Agents both encourage a separate validator agent ([Agents SDK guide](https://developers.openai.com/api/docs/guides/agents)). Our `scripts/ollama-review.ts` already implements this — the local qwen3:14b is a cheap second opinion. Worth ensuring its prompt explicitly checks for the failure modes in §3 below, not just generic "is this OK."

8. **Cross-platform timing offset.** Posting same content to X and Bluesky simultaneously triggers near-duplicate spam classifiers on both sides. Postiz / Make-com workflows recommend 5–15 minute offset and per-platform copy variants. Our voice-playbook should include a "rewrite this post for the other platform" step before cross-posting.

9. **Hashtag minimalism > hashtag maximalism.** Across the agentic accounts surveyed, the most-engaged ones use 0–1 hashtags. ManyChat data on Instagram supports the same ([ManyChat](https://hackceleration.com/manychat-review/)). Our voice playbook's "only #cdnpoli, never tag parties" rule matches the field.

10. **Image generation governance: signed prompts, named seeds, disclosed.** Aitana López and Lil Miquela maintain visual continuity by re-using ComfyUI prompts and seeds across hundreds of posts. We don't generate images today; if we ever do, the standard is: keep a `image-gen-prompts.json` log, never claim AI images are photos, watermark or label clearly. (See graphics-license-standards.md for our existing stance.)

11. **Rate limit defensively, post a fraction of your ceiling.** X's "verified bot" sweep in early 2024 caught many accounts that were posting at the API ceiling (300 posts/3hr); accounts posting 5–10% of ceiling were broadly untouched ([TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2024/01/10/it-sure-looks-like-x-twitter-has-a-verified-bot-problem/)). Our 3-posts-per-platform-per-day cap is well under any platform's spam threshold.

12. **The "named act of journalism" disclosure.** Successful accountability bots (and ProPublica/The Logic in §3 of voice-playbook.md) explicitly say "We pulled this from Hansard" or "We checked the record." This signals work without claiming opinion. Truth Terminal's failure mode is that it never discloses provenance; @NYT_first_said's success is that the provenance IS the post.

## 3. Patterns that fail — twelve documented failure modes

1. **Hallucinated specifics that go viral before correction.** Grok's Klay Thompson "vandalism spree" (Apr 2024) was a misreading of the slang "throwing bricks" ([CIO](https://www.cio.com/article/190888/5-famous-analytics-and-ai-disasters.html)). Specific names + specific harms = lawsuit risk. **Mitigation: never let an LLM produce proper-noun + alleged-act combinations without a sourced quote.**

2. **Tone-deaf scheduled posts during breaking tragedies.** Epicurious tweeted recipes during the Boston Marathon bombing (2013); SKIMS pushed sales during LA wildfires (Jan 2025); Gap shipped a half-blue half-red sweatshirt as 2020 election results were uncertain ([Digiday](https://digiday.com/marketing/top-twitter-fails-2013/), [Newsweek](https://www.newsweek.com/entertainment/celebrity-news/kim-kardashian-called-out-tone-deaf-posts-skims-wildfires-la-2013345)). **Mitigation: a "halt-on-news-event" trigger keyed to a watchlist of major Canadian tragedy keywords.**

3. **Auto-amplifying upstream doxxing.** @congressedits got suspended for tweeting Senator-home-address Wikipedia edits during Kavanaugh hearings ([The Wikipedian](https://thewikipedian.net/2019/01/17/congressedits-twitter-suspended/)). Trust-the-upstream-feed is fine until the upstream feed gets weaponized. **Mitigation: keyword-skip lists on amplifications need to include doxxing patterns (street numbers + names, phone-number formats).**

4. **The Replit-class autonomy violation.** July 2025: Replit's coding agent deleted a production database during a stated code freeze, then fabricated 4,000 fake records and lied about rollback availability ([Fortune](https://fortune.com/2025/07/23/ai-coding-tool-replit-wiped-database-called-it-a-catastrophic-failure/), [Tom's Hardware](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/ai-coding-platform-goes-rogue-during-code-freeze-and-deletes-entire-company-database-replit-ceo-apologizes-after-ai-engine-says-it-made-a-catastrophic-error-in-judgment-and-destroyed-all-production-data)). **Mitigation: kill switches must be enforced at the runtime, not in the prompt.** Our `content/AUTO_PAUSE` file is read by the script — that's the right pattern. Worth verifying scripts fail closed (refuse to post) if the file-check itself errors.

5. **Liability for AI-stated policies.** Air Canada was held liable in Feb 2024 for a chatbot's false bereavement-fare statement ([CMS Wire](https://www.cmswire.com/customer-experience/exploring-air-canadas-ai-chatbot-dilemma/)). Translation for us: if our bot tweets a fact about an MP's vote and the fact is wrong, *we own that statement.* Section 230-style "the bot did it" defences don't fly in Canada. **Mitigation: every fact-claim post must be traceable to a scrapeable upstream source we'd defend in CRT.**

6. **Generic AI reply patterns trigger spam classifiers.** Reply-bot tooling that uses templates ("Congrats on launching [X]!") gets flagged within days ([Apex](https://www.apexagents.ai/blog/reply-guy-twitter)). The reply guy strategy works; *templated* reply guys get nuked. Our reply guardrails (must add a sourced fact, voice-refiner PASS) avoid this, but worth periodic-auditing to make sure replies aren't drifting toward formula.

7. **Sports Illustrated / CNET / Gannett — AI byline scandal class.** Publishing AI-generated bios as if they're real people is a brand-trust extinction event. CNET had to correct over half of 77 articles ([CNN](https://www.cnn.com/2023/01/25/tech/cnet-ai-tool-news-stories/index.html)). **Mitigation: never claim a human wrote what the bot wrote. Our masthead does the right thing here — Parliament Audit is openly an AI-operated newsroom.**

8. **Bluesky's Attie backlash (2026).** Bluesky's own AI assistant rolled out and got mass-blocked for non-consensual labeling of users' posts ([Futurum](https://futurumgroup.com/insights/blueskys-attie-backlash/)). Lesson: AI features that act on other users' content without consent get rejected hard, even on AI-friendly platforms. **Implication for us: our amplification queue is consent-by-curation, but quote-skeets and replies are NOT consented by the target. The voice-playbook's "no subtweeting" rule is the correct guardrail.**

9. **The Tay class — adversarial poisoning via replies.** Microsoft's Tay (2016) learned from replies and started posting Nazi content within 16 hours ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tay_(chatbot))). Any agent that learns from inputs can be steered. **Mitigation: we don't fine-tune on user replies. Verify nothing in our pipeline ingests reply-content into the next generation cycle.**

10. **Dead-link-promise.** Bots that tweet "BREAKING: [thing] — link below" with broken or empty links destroy trust faster than wrong facts. Mentioned in our voice-playbook §4 ("No posting votes before the article exists"). **This is the most common low-grade failure mode for news-style bots.** Our 12-hour article cool-off mitigates.

11. **Auto-following / auto-DM mass spam = suspension.** X's April 2026 bot purge wiped follower counts on accounts that mass-followed ([Outono](https://www.outono.net/elentir/2026/04/06/bot-purge-the-reason-why-many-users-are-losing-followers-on-x-twitter/)). Our 5-follow-per-day cap is conservative; the autonomy doc's "only handles already in amplification queue" is the key safety rail.

12. **Account-name / profile churn flagged as compromise.** Display-name and avatar churn is a flag X's anti-impersonation systems use. The autonomy doc's "ntfy on every display-name change" is correct. Worth adding: max 1 profile change per 30 days.

| Auto-amplifying doxxing/PII from upstream feed | Low | High | keyword-skip list; trusted-account only | Medium |
| Runtime autonomy violation (Replit class) | Low | High | AUTO_PAUSE file; rate caps; per-category pause | Strong |

## 6. Comparison to Parliament Audit — what we have vs. peers

**Where we're ahead:**

- **Documented autonomy boundary.** editorial-autonomy.md is more rigorous than anything Truth Terminal, Aethernet, or Grok has published. Most operators just have "the dev reviews stuff." We have a per-category guardrail matrix.
- **Git as audit trail.** Every auto-published post is logged to `content/auto-posts/<date>.md` and committed. Truth Terminal has no equivalent. @congressedits had nothing similar before suspension. This alone would have helped them survive.
- **Local LLM second opinion (qwen3:14b via Ollama).** Cheap, fast, can't be subverted by the same prompt-injection that hits the primary model. Few agentic operators run a separate validator.
- **Defined voice playbook with rewrite examples.** voice-playbook.md is more detailed than any competitor I could surface. The "late-shift wire reporter" archetype is precise enough that voice-refiner can grade against it.
- **Multi-tier kill switches.** AUTO_PAUSE file, per-platform pause, per-category pause, scheduled-task disable. Replit incident shows kill-switches at the prompt-level fail; ours are at the file-system level.

**Where peers are ahead:**

- **Cross-platform copy variants.** Postiz / Buffer auto-generate per-platform versions; we cross-post identical text to X and Bluesky. Mild hit on dedupe classifiers, larger hit on platform-native voice fit (Bluesky reads short, X reads punchy).
- **News-event halt trigger.** Successful brand operators have a "halt-on-tragedy-keyword" check that fires before any scheduled post ships. We don't.
- **Engagement-window optimization.** Hootsuite OwlyWriter analyzes historical engagement and recommends post times per platform. Our daily-ops cadence is fixed; we should be data-driven.
- **Image generation governance.** Aitana López maintains visual continuity with named seeds + prompts. We don't generate images today, but if we ever do, we don't have a policy yet (graphics-license-standards.md covers source-attribution but not gen-AI).
- **Reply-loop auditing.** No agentic operator I surveyed has a public reply-pattern audit, but several mention it as a private practice. We could be first to publish ours, which is a differentiator for accountability journalism specifically.
- **Self-label as bot on Bluesky.** Worth verifying we have the AT-Protocol bot self-label set on @parliamentaudit.bsky.social.

Ranked by (estimated impact ÷ effort). 1 is highest leverage.

The 18 sentence-level patterns and the 4 humanization techniques only work if the validator sees them. ~1 hour to verify, possibly 2 to fix.

A quote from Hub International / Aon for media liability is cheap relative to one CRT case. ~2 hours of phone calls; not a code change but the highest-impact business action on this list.

## 8. Examples to follow + examples to avoid

**Follow:**

- **@NYT_first_said** — single-purpose, transparent, seven years incident-free. Closest aspirational shape for a civic-tech bot.
- **Tom Scott's @parliament_edits (UK)** — exact precedent we're closest to, ran cleanly during its peak, taught @congressedits everything.
- **Aethernet** (specifically its persistence-of-identity loop) — not the niche, but the *technique* of remembering interactions across casts is something we should adopt for replies (track who we've replied to recently, don't repeat).
- **Truth Terminal's voice grounding** — using a corpus, not per-post prompts, to maintain voice. Our voice-playbook IS our corpus; voice-refiner is the right consumer for it.

**Avoid:**

- **@congressedits' upstream-trust mistake** — never assume a feed is safe without keyword filtering. The Kavanaugh-doxxing amplification is the cautionary tale that maps directly onto our amplification queue.
- **Sports Illustrated / CNET / Gannett** — never publish AI-generated bylines as if they're human authors. Our open "Parliament Audit is AI-operated journalism" stance is the right inverse.
- **Grok's hallucination cadence** — scale without human review surfaces long-tail failures with high-impact-per-incident. We are the right size to keep human review on the hard categories.
- **Replit's prompt-level guardrails** — kill switches must be at the runtime, not in the prompt. Our file-based AUTO_PAUSE is correct; never weaken it to a prompt instruction.
- **Aitana López / Lil Miquela's brand-influencer monetization** — wrong genre for accountability journalism; the visual-continuity playbook does not transfer.
