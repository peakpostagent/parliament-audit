## 1. The project: PrescribeIT, not a generic "health IT" file

The "$300M federal health-care software" referenced by the source is almost certainly **PrescribeIT**, a national e-prescribing platform run by Canada Health Infoway, a federally funded not-for-profit. Key facts:

- **Total federal spending:** Initially reported as ~$250M; Health Canada disclosed at committee on April 21, 2026 that the actual figure is **$298M** since 2017. Conservative MPs round this to "$300M." Some Conservative communications use "~$300-million" verbatim (Mazier, on April 27).
- **Vendor:** Telus Health was the design and operations partner, paid roughly **$98M**. Telus retained ~**85% of the underlying intellectual property** — meaning PrescribeIT could not be transferred or maintained without Telus, and the federal government holds no IP rights (per a Health Canada response to an order paper question).
- **Uptake:** Below 5% of Canadian prescriptions — the program failed at scale.
- **Wind-down:** Canada Health Infoway announced on Feb 18, 2026 the program will end **May 29, 2026**, transitioning to "an open-standards approach."
- **Slogan:** Sometimes described in media as the "axe the fax" project.

The user's "$300M health-care software" framing therefore maps cleanly to PrescribeIT.

## 2. The committee: Standing Committee on Health (HESA)

**HESA** — the House of Commons Standing Committee on Health — has carriage of the file. Hearings have been webcast live, not in camera. The committee chair is **Hedy Fry** (Liberal, Vancouver Centre), elected to the chair after the 2025 election.

Other committees relevant to context but not currently the lead:
- PACP (Public Accounts) is the body most likely to inherit the file IF the Auditor General accepts the Conservative request for a probe. As of April 28, 2026 there is no public indication the AG has agreed.
- OGGO and INDU have not picked up the file in this session.

## 4. Meeting 29 (Apr 21) — what happened publicly

This was the on-the-record session, not in camera. Witnesses included:
- **Michael Green** — CEO, Canada Health Infoway
- Officials from **Health Canada**
- **Ratcho Batchvarov** — VP Provider Solutions, Telus Health

Recorded on the public webcast:
- Liberal chair Hedy Fry asked Green directly: "Can you tell us what was wrong?"
- Liberal MP **Doug Eyolfson**: "Why weren't physicians using this? Had problems been identified?"
- Conservative MP **Helena Konanz** asked Green for his 2025 salary; Green declined to provide a figure despite acknowledging it is publicly disclosed.
- Telus VP Batchvarov disclosed the 85%-IP figure and stated PrescribeIT could not be transferred or maintained without Telus.
- Green could not account for the remaining federal funds; offered to provide board reports later.

The committee voted **unanimously** at the end of Meeting 29 to summon Green, the Infoway board chair, and a Telus Health representative for further testimony, with a target return date of May 6, 2026.

**Important:** there is no public record of any in-camera motion at Meeting 29. The full meeting was webcast.

## 5. Meeting 31 (Apr 28) — the procedural showdown

This is the meeting that most plausibly underlies the user's report. Documented facts:

- Meeting was **convened under Standing Order 106(4)** — the rule that requires a chair to call a committee meeting within five days when at least four members request it in writing. SO 106(4) is a Conservative tool for forcing a meeting when the chair would otherwise sit on the file.
- Two formal agenda items: **"Election of Chair"** and discussion of the **Committee's PrescribeIT investigation**.
- Format: televised on the public listing — but a televised meeting can still go in camera by majority vote at any point.
- Duration: only 57 minutes (12:03–1:00 p.m.), unusually short for a contested meeting.

**Why a chair election now?** Liberals passed the House motion restructuring committees late on Monday Apr 27, 2026, expanding most committees from 4 Lib / 4 Con / 1 Bloc to **7 Lib / 4 Con / 1 Bloc**. New Liberal members on HESA on Apr 28 would have the votes to:
1. Replace the chair (although Hedy Fry is already a Liberal — re-election may have been pro-forma confirmation under the new composition);
2. Decline to authorize Conservative-requested document production;
3. Vote to take the meeting in camera.

**As of this writing (Apr 28, 2026 evening), the official minutes of Meeting 31 are not yet posted.** openparliament.ca confirms transcripts can take up to 10 days. The webcast recording exists on Parlvu/ourcommons but I have not been able to verify its content directly, and no major news outlet has yet published a write-up of the Apr 28 HESA meeting outcome.

## 6. Did Liberals use their new majority to go in camera?

**Verified:**
- Liberals achieved House majority on Apr 28 with three byelection MPs sworn in (Doly Begum, Danielle Martin, Tatiana Auguste), combined with the four Conservative floor-crossings (d'Entremont, Ma, Jeneroux, Gladu).
- Liberals passed the committee restructuring motion **late Monday night Apr 27, 2026**, after invoking time allocation earlier the same day. Conservative House Leader Andrew Scheer: "The very first vote that these new Liberal MPs will pronounce on will be a vote to shut down debate."
- Conservative MP **Dan Mazier** (Conservative health critic), speaking to reporters Apr 27: Conservatives "have been working at the committee level to produce documents related to PrescribeIT" and accused the government of "filibustering those efforts until it can restructure the parliamentary committees to reflect the Liberals' new majority." Mazier warned: "if that happens, the public may never see documents explaining the government's handling of PrescribeIT."
- HESA Meeting 31 occurred Apr 28, 2026 with a chair-election + SO 106(4) PrescribeIT-investigation agenda.

**Not yet verified:**
- A specific recorded in-camera motion at HESA on Apr 28 (or any later date).
- Vote count, mover, or motion text.
- Whether Liberals replaced the chair, retained Fry, or used the new majority composition for some other procedural purpose.

The user's claim — that "the Liberals used their new House majority to vote to go in camera" — is **plausible and consistent** with Mazier's public warning, the procedural timing, and the Liberals' demonstrated willingness to use majority leverage on the same day to limit debate in the House. But it is **not yet documented** in any source I can independently verify.

It is equally possible that:
- Meeting 31 went in camera on Liberal motion to discuss committee business (a routine framing) but produced no decision unfriendly to PrescribeIT scrutiny;
- The meeting concluded without an in-camera vote and the user is conflating Mazier's public allegation of "filibustering" with an in-camera vote;
- The in-camera vote took place but happens to be unreported because committee in-camera proceedings are routinely unrecorded and the meeting concluded only hours before this research was compiled.

## 10. Confidence and gaps

**Confidence: MEDIUM.**

What I can defend in print right now:
- The committee is HESA. (HIGH)
- The project is PrescribeIT, a $298M (~$300M) federal e-prescribing program. (HIGH)
- A substantive PrescribeIT briefing took place on Apr 21, 2026 (Meeting 29). (HIGH)
- A follow-on procedural meeting requested under SO 106(4) took place on Apr 28, 2026 (Meeting 31), under the Liberals' new committee majority. (HIGH)
- The Liberal House majority motion to restructure committees passed late Monday Apr 27. (HIGH)
- Conservative MP Dan Mazier publicly warned Apr 27 that Liberals were filibustering production-of-documents on PrescribeIT and that the new majority threatened to bury the file. (HIGH)
