# Press Coverage Research — Floor Crossings (2026)

Research notes for the inaugural "How the press covered this week" column. Covers Canadian media framing of the four Conservative-to-Liberal floor crossings that handed Mark Carney a majority without a general election: Chris d'Entremont (Nov 4, 2025), Michael Ma (Dec 11, 2025), Matt Jeneroux (Feb 18, 2026), Marilyn Gladu (Apr 8, 2026).

Researched 2026-04-21. All URLs verified from live search results; article framing drawn from accessible fetches and search-result summaries. Where an outlet's specific coverage could not be confirmed, gaps are explicitly flagged.

## Gaps / patterns observed

- **The byelection question is the fault line.** Every right-leaning outlet (Rebel News, Brian Lilley/Postmedia, the Sarnia Journal opinion column) leads with Gladu's January byelection pledge as a hypocrisy hook. Most mainstream outlets (CBC news, Globe news, CTV, Petrolia Independent) mention it — but buried or framed as "one of several reactions," not as the story. The Tyee, the Hill Times opinion page, and Brad Wall's Globe op-ed critique the *practice* of floor-crossing without centering the specific pledge.
- **Outlets favorable to Carney center majority mechanics; outlets hostile to Carney center democratic legitimacy.** CBC analysis frames the crossings as Carney's magnetism ("attracting support from unlikely places"). The Hill Times' convention write-up treats "big tent" as the organizing frame. Rebel, Lilley, and the Hill Times op-ed treat "betrays the will of the voters" as the organizing frame.
- **D'Entremont got personal-narrative framing; Gladu got hypocrisy framing.** D'Entremont's November crossing was covered primarily through his own stated motives (Poilievre's tone, the "snake" incident, the budget). Gladu's April crossing was covered primarily through contradiction with her own past words. By the fourth crossing, the media frame had shifted from "why this MP" to "why this system."
- **Local papers split sharply.** The Sarnia Journal broke the hypocrisy story and ran the single sharpest rhetorical piece in the corpus ("hijacked our riding"). The Petrolia Lambton Independent, covering the same riding, ran Gladu's own framing largely unchallenged. Two papers, same geography, opposite editorial instincts.
- **The cumulative-majority angle is almost absent.** Only a handful of pieces (Hill Times, the Conversation via Tyee-adjacent republications, Globe "scale" op-ed) treat the four crossings as a single story about minority-to-majority conversion without an election. Most outlets covered each crossing as an isolated event, which blunts the democratic-legitimacy critique.
- **Notable verified gaps:** No Toronto Star article surfaced; no iPolitics piece surfaced; no Blacklock's article surfaced (paywall); no Halifax Examiner on d'Entremont; Canadaland's Short Cuts did not produce a confirmable dedicated segment. Worth manual verification before filing.
