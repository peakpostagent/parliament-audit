# Parliament Audit — Competitor & Comparable Account Analysis

**Prepared:** April 16, 2026  
**Purpose:** Identify 10 accounts doing similar work to @ParliamentAudit, analyze what makes them effective, and extract actionable tactics to improve our reach.

---

## Executive Summary

We reviewed 10 accounts across civic journalism, government transparency, polling, data visualization, and political accountability. The key takeaway: **the accounts with the best follower-to-post ratios don't just share information — they present it in a format that's instantly shareable.** Standardized visual templates, native content (not just links), and a clear brand voice are the three things every successful account in this space has in common.

### Top 5 Tactics to Implement Immediately

1. **Standardized visual templates** — Every post should have a branded chart, card, or stat graphic (we've started this with OG images and chart routes — expand it)
2. **Thread strategy** — Break articles into 4-5 tweet threads with a hook, not just a link to the site
3. **Reply strategy (70/30 rule)** — 70% of activity should be replies to larger accounts in #cdnpoli, 30% original posts
4. **Polling/engagement posts** — Ask questions ("Did you know...?", polls) to drive replies, which the algorithm weights 15x over likes
5. **Consistent posting cadence** — 1-3 posts per day, same time slots, never go dark

## The 10 Accounts

### 1. @CanadianPolling — Polling Canada
**Followers:** 104.2K | **Posts:** 24.4K | **Category:** Canadian polling aggregator

**What they do:**  
Shares every major Canadian poll as it drops — federal, provincial, byelections. Standardized format: party abbreviation, percentage, change from last election. References @338Canada for model projections. Monetizes through Ko-fi donations and a Substack newsletter for subscribers who want deeper model access.

**What they do well:**
- **Standardized post format** — Every poll looks the same. Followers know exactly what they're getting. This builds recognition and makes rapid scanning easy.
- **Speed** — They're often the first to post a new poll, which captures the "breaking" engagement window.
- **Cross-referencing** — Tagging @338Canada in every post creates a network effect where both accounts grow together.
- **Monetization path** — Free data on X, paid models on Substack. Smart funnel.

**What we can emulate:**
- Create a standardized "RECORDED VOTE" template with consistent formatting (we've drafted this — execute it consistently)
- Be the fastest to post when a division happens in the House
- Cross-reference other Canadian civic accounts to build network effects
- Consider a free/paid split: free vote results on X, detailed analysis on parliamentaudit.ca

### 2. @ByDonkeys — Led By Donkeys
**Followers:** 906.7K | **Posts:** ~2,600 | **Category:** UK political accountability campaign

**What they do:**  
A UK accountability project that holds politicians to their own words. Started during Brexit by putting politicians' old tweets on billboards, then filming the reactions. Evolved into a full-blown media operation with video stunts, projections onto buildings, and crowdfunded campaigns. Raised nearly £500K from 16,000+ supporters.

**What they do well:**
- **Extraordinary follower-to-post ratio** — 906K followers from only ~2,600 posts. That's ~349 followers per post. Most accounts are lucky to get 1-2.
- **Video-first content** — Almost everything is a video or image. Native media, not links.
- **Accountability framing** — "Here's what they said. Here's what they did." Simple, powerful, shareable.
- **Crowdfunding model** — Community-funded, which builds loyalty and ownership.
- **Low frequency, high impact** — They don't post 10 times a day. They post when they have something worth sharing.

**What we can emulate:**
- The "said vs. did" format is directly applicable to our Accountability Check template — lean into it harder
- Invest in visual/video content. Even simple quote cards with contrasting statements would work.
- Quality over quantity. One devastating fact-check post will outperform 10 link-share posts.
- Consider crowdfunding to build community investment in the project

### 3. @USAFacts
**Followers:** 260.7K | **Posts:** 15.5K | **Category:** US government data, non-partisan

**What they do:**  
Founded by Steve Ballmer. Takes government data and makes it accessible. Covers spending, immigration, economy, education — anything with public data. Posts 1-2 times per day with branded charts, infographics, and short videos. Strictly non-partisan. Gold checkmark (verified org).

**What they do well:**
- **Branded visual identity** — Pink/magenta brand color across all visual assets. USAFacts logo watermarked on every chart. Instantly recognizable in a feed.
- **Multiple video series with distinct personalities** — "Just the Facts with Steve Ballmer" (celebrity/founder), "Nate's Weird Government Facts" (casual/fun), and "Just a Fact" (another presenter). Humanizes the data.
- **News-hook posting** — They tie data to current events and calendar moments (Tax Day, inflation reports). Timely = shareable.
- **Question-based engagement prompts** — Posts often end with a question ("What are your thoughts on the current corporate tax structure?").
- **Humor and relatability** — Light, conversational tone even with serious data. Makes numbers approachable.

**Important caveat — same low engagement problem as Pew:**
- Typical posts: 5-14 likes, 0-3 retweets, 1.4K-3.2K views, 0-1 replies
- That's under 0.01% engagement rate on 260K followers
- Their pinned post (352 likes, 120K views) is a massive outlier — normal posts get almost no interaction
- Lesson reinforced: **large institutional accounts broadcasting data charts without community engagement get views but not interaction**

**What we can emulate:**
- Develop a locked-in visual brand for charts (we've started with the OG chart route — expand to more chart types)
- Repurpose each article into multiple formats: tweet, thread, chart image, and website article
- Lead with the most surprising number, not the bill number
- The question-based hook at the end of posts is worth testing
- Their video series idea (different personalities for different content types) is smart — consider eventually
- But again: **don't just broadcast — engage.** Their low engagement rate proves that charts alone don't build community

### 4. @OpenSecretsDC — OpenSecrets
**Followers:** 153.1K | **Posts:** ~40K | **Category:** US money-in-politics transparency

**What they do:**  
Track every dollar in American politics — campaign donations, lobbying, PACs, dark money. Produce data-rich reports and visualizations. Weekly newsletter breaks down money flows.

**What they do well:**
- **"Follow the money" niche** — They own one specific angle (money in politics) and are the definitive source for it.
- **Timely hooks** — When a politician does something newsworthy, they immediately post the financial context ("This senator received $X from [industry] before voting on [bill]")
- **Database as content** — Their website IS the content. The X account drives traffic to searchable databases.
- **Institutional credibility** — Cited by journalists constantly, which creates a virtuous cycle of awareness.

**What we can emulate:**
- Own the "voting record" niche in Canada the way OpenSecrets owns money-in-politics in the US
- When a vote happens, immediately post the context: who voted how, and what their past positions were
- Build the website into a searchable, citable database that journalists will reference
- Make it easy for reporters to cite us — structured data, embed codes, clear sourcing

### 5. @338Canada — Philippe J. Fournier
**Followers:** 45.9K | **Posts:** 19.2K | **Category:** Canadian electoral projections

**What they do:**  
Statistical model of Canadian electoral projections based on polls, electoral history, and demographics. Run by Philippe J. Fournier from Montreal. Writes for L'actualité, The Walrus, and Politico Ottawa. Co-hosts election night livestreams on YouTube. Posts 3-5 times per day.

**Best engagement rate of any Canadian account we reviewed:**
- Typical posts: 50-200 likes, 10-70 retweets, 3K-10K views
- Breakout posts (Quebec polls, election results): **500 likes, 149 retweets, 64K views**
- That's a ~1% engagement rate on 46K followers — dramatically better than Pew (416K) or USAFacts (261K)

**What they do well:**
- **Personal brand = institutional brand** — The account IS Philippe Fournier. His name, face, credentials, and personality make it feel human and authoritative. This is why engagement is so high.
- **Structured data formatting** — Uses colored emoji circles for poll numbers (🔴 LPC 45%, 🔵 CPC 33%). Scannable, consistent, instantly recognizable.
- **Source attribution transparency** — Always cites polling firm, date, sample size, MOE. Builds credibility with every post.
- **Bilingual posting** — Posts in both English and French, expanding reach across both language communities.
- **Thread strategy for complex data** — Breaks multi-data-point posts into threads rather than cramming into one tweet.
- **Media cross-pollination** — Writes for L'actualité, The Walrus, Politico Ottawa. Each article drives followers back to X.
- **Symbiotic relationship with @CanadianPolling** — Both accounts reference each other constantly, growing together.
- **Livestream events** — Co-hosts election night coverage on YouTube, promoted via X. Positions him as the go-to real-time analyst.
- **Tags polling firms** — Quotes and tags firms like @pallasdata, which earns reciprocal engagement from their networks.

**What we can emulate:**
- Consider building a personal brand alongside @ParliamentAudit (founder's face/voice adds trust — this is THE differentiator for engagement)
- Use colored emoji formatting for vote results (🟢 YEA 186 🔴 NAY 137) — scannable and recognizable
- Always cite source, date, and methodology — this builds trust with every single post
- Publish our methodology prominently (we have /methodology — promote it more)
- Build the relationship: reply to @338Canada and @CanadianPolling posts with added vote context
- Consider bilingual content for Quebec reach
- Seek media mentions and guest writing opportunities to cross-pollinate audiences

**What they do:**  
The closest US equivalent to Parliament Audit. Tracked every bill and vote in Congress since 2004. Sent email alerts when Congress voted. Open-source civic tech project.

**What this means for us:**
- There's a proven 82K-follower audience for vote-tracking content — and nobody is serving them on X anymore

- Automated vote alerts should be our bread and butter (this was their core product)
- Visual bill progress trackers would be engaging content for threads
- Consider open-sourcing parts of the platform to build community and credibility

### 10. @pewresearch — Pew Research Center
**Followers:** 416.3K | **Posts:** 103.2K | **Category:** Non-partisan research and data

**What they do:**  
The gold standard for non-partisan research communication. Cover politics, media, technology, demographics. Post 5-8 times per day. Pure broadcast model — they follow only 75 accounts and don't engage in replies.

**What they do well:**
- **Branded chart images are the core product** — Nearly every post features a polished, watermarked "PEW RESEARCH CENTER" data visualization. Bar charts, line charts, dot plots, scatter plots. Instantly recognizable.
- **Multi-post topic clusters** — They break a single research report into 3-4 sequential posts: a question hook, 1-2 data charts, then a link to the full report. Not formal threads, but sequential posts that maximize impressions per study.
- **Question-based hooks** — Their highest-engagement post opened with "How religious do you think Donald Trump is?" paired with a photo. Questions dramatically outperform pure data posts.
- **Newsjacking** — They tie research to trending news (Trump, Amazon, TikTok). Posts referencing current events consistently get higher engagement.
- **Neutral framing** — Never editorialize. Present findings and let the audience react. Shareable across the political spectrum.

**Important caveat — low engagement rate:**
- Despite 416K followers, typical posts get only 2-40 likes and 0-25 retweets (under 0.01% engagement rate)
- Views range from 1.5K-6.7K per post — modest for that follower count
- This is a pure broadcast model with no community-building (no polls, no reply engagement, no quote tweets)
- Lesson: **big follower count ≠ engagement.** A smaller account with genuine community interaction can outperform them post-for-post.

**What we can emulate:**
- The "X% of Canadians..." question format is the most effective hook — their best post used it
- Every data post should have a branded visual. Period. No text-only posts for data stories.
- Multi-post clusters: hook question → chart → chart → full report link
- Invest in a consistent chart style that becomes synonymous with Parliament Audit
- Their neutral framing is exactly what we should aspire to
- But **don't copy their broadcast-only model** — engage with replies and build community, which they fail to do

## Key Insight

The gap in the Canadian market is enormous. @openparlca has the data but no social media game. @CanadianPolling has the audience but only covers polls. @338Canada has the models but only covers elections. **Nobody is doing what Parliament Audit does: editorial, visual, non-partisan coverage of every parliamentary vote, designed for social media.** The opportunity is to become the @pewresearch of Canadian parliamentary transparency — authoritative, visual, shareable, and relentlessly consistent.

The single biggest lever for growth right now: **be in people's replies.** The 70/30 rule (70% replies to larger accounts, 30% original content) is how small accounts break through the algorithm. Every reply to a @CanadianPolling poll post or a journalist's take on a vote is a chance to put Parliament Audit in front of their audience.
